## Design Context

### Users
Senior Dart and Flutter engineers building apps that have grown into platforms. They reach for Plugin Kit at the moment a DI container starts feeling thin: when behavior needs to be replaced, layered, disabled, overridden, or vetoed at runtime. They are skeptical of marketing, allergic to magic, and read code before they read copy. Designs are seen on GitHub, pub.dev, Medium, and the docs site, usually in a dark editor or browser tab. The job: convince an experienced engineer that this runtime is serious infrastructure they can stake a codebase on.

### Brand Personality
Precise. Confident. Industrial. Quiet dry wit when it shows up (the README's "the word 'just' starts lying to you"). Never cute, never cheerful, never apologetic. Voice is the senior engineer who has shipped this before and is telling you what they learned. Emotional target: trust and competence first, with a thin top-note of "this was actually nice to work with."

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first. The brand palette is yellow (#F6E41B), gold (#F6C800), deep brown (#965F00), with a low warm-tone (#3A2E0A) and sister-package blues (#39CEFD, #16B9FD, #03569B) reserved for differentiating per-package marks. Backdrops are warm dark, never pure black; warmth ties the yellow logos to the surface instead of letting them float. Typography is Inter (already in use); monospace lockups only where they earn it.

Avoid: generic SaaS purple-to-pink gradients, glassmorphism, AI-startup glow, crypto/web3 neon and hexagons, cheerful mascots or pastel friendliness, plastic 3D bevels, glossy reflective floors, and big-title marketing-template layouts.

Reach for: composed symmetry, generous negative space, one strong focal point with quieter supporting elements, subtle warm halos instead of saturated glows, hairline rules over heavy borders, and texture that feels machined (fine grids, subtle grain) rather than decorative.

### Design Principles
1. Confidence through restraint. Strong choices, few of them. If something is decorative, cut it.
2. Warm dark, never pure black. The backdrop carries warmth so the brand yellow reads as material, not neon.
3. One focal point. The all-yellow mark anchors; sister marks support at a lower visual weight.
4. Engineered, not decorated. Geometry, symmetry, and alignment do the work. No flourishes that a code reviewer would call gratuitous.
5. Earn every label. Text on a brand surface is justified by identification or tagline duty, not atmosphere.
